Overview

 

JCAT uses dynamic Bayesian networks (DBN) to estimate the states of these decisions or assets over time.  JCAT represents the DBNs by a graphical modeling approach.  Each model is represented as a graph or diagram. The model’s random variables (Events) are shown as rectangles with rounded edges, called nodes, and the conditional dependencies (Mechanisms) are shown as arrows, or directed arcs, between variables.

 

The user “draws” the actions and mechanisms in the model layout frame; a button on the Controls toolbar initiates the Bayesian network calculations.  The color-coded output provides the user with a visual prediction of the input model.  Models are saved to and loaded from text-based files using the extension .JCAT.  

 

JCAT is developed and maintained, under the direction of Dr. John Lemmer, by the Air Force Research Lab (AFRL) – Rome Research Site (RRS), in Rome, NY.
