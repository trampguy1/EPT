About Bayesian Networks

 

Bayesian belief networks and inference is a branch of mathematical probability theory that allows us to model uncertainty about the world and outcomes of interest by combining common-sense knowledge and observational evidence.

 

A Bayesian network is essentially a directed graph.  Vertices in the graph represent objects and events in the world, and edges represent direct causal relationships.  In addition, nodes must have associated Conditional Probability Tables (CPTs) in order for probability calculations to be carried out.

 

JCAT uses dynamic Bayesian networks to estimate the states of these decisions or assets over time.  After the Bayes Net has been built you can view the probability of success for each time-step using the probability profiles view.
